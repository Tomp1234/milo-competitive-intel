# Milo GTM Plan

## Goal
12 closed deals by end of Q1 2026. Currently at 4 paying customers (BetGames, Perlas/FrontIT, Orbio, 7Bet). MaxWellStone is a reseller, not counted toward target.

## High-Level Decisions

### Go-to-Market Approach: Phone-First Outbound + Email Sequences
- **What:** Cold calling as primary channel, email sequences as supporting channel (now booking meetings).
- **Why:** 1.0% meeting rate from calls remains the primary driver. Email sequences now also producing meetings (Coolr + Black Sheep Coffee booked via Liam's sequences, Feb 2026) - may be viable with better targeting/copy than early Q4 2025 testing.
- **What we're NOT doing:** Content marketing, paid ads, inbound-led growth. Too slow or ineffective for current stage.

### Sales Methodology: Benjamin Dennehy Framework
- **What**: Disqualification-first approach. Emotional grinder questioning. Push-away techniques. Lead with pain, not features.
- **Why**: Separates genuine interest from casual browsing. Gets to real pain faster than consultative selling. Works well with Milo's positioning (solving frustration, not adding features).
- **Key elements**: Pattern interrupt → 30-second choice → Three pain points → Objection handling via curiosity → Push-away close

### Target Profile: Business Leaders Who Feel Data Pain, Not Technical Builders
- **What**: Heads of Sales, COOs, CEOs at 100-500 employee companies, Commercial Directors - people who WAIT for data, not people who MANAGE data.
- **Why**: Technical buyers (BI Managers, Heads of Analytics) often think "we could build this" or get lost in architecture discussions. Business leaders feel the pain of waiting days for answers and make faster decisions.
- **Disqualify**: Large engineering teams, "we're building our own" language, technical validators who want to poke architecture before understanding value, UK subsidiaries of US companies.

### Positioning: "ChatGPT for Business, But Safe"
- **What**: Lead with security concern, not analytics capability
- **Why**: Companies reaching out cite security fears about employees uploading data to personal ChatGPT. This is the door-opener. The analytics value is the reason they stay.
- **Proof point**: Microsoft Copilot failures creating opportunity - prospects disappointed it can't explain causation.

## Non-Negotiables

### Weekly Activity Minimums
- 800-1000 cold calls per week (combined team: 400-500 each Tom + Liam)
- Email sequences running in parallel (now booking meetings - Coolr + Black Sheep Coffee via Liam)
- 4+ discovery calls per week
- Why: Pipeline math. At 1.0% meeting rate, 800-1000 calls = 8-10 meetings/week potential.

### POC Success Criteria Before Conversion
- Active usage without prompting (not just when we check in)
- Trust improving - BetGames and Perlas expanding usage across teams
- Champion who will advocate internally
- Why: BetGames shows the risk - paying but not all users active. Usage must be real before renewal.

### Disqualification Speed
- Kill bad-fit conversations fast. Don't chase technical validators.
- Why: Time is the constraint. Every hour on a bad prospect is an hour not spent on a good one.

## Target Verticals (Ranked)

1. **eCommerce/DTC** - Primary focus. Large addressable market. Data typically well-structured, faster time-to-value.
2. **Event Ticketing** - Emerging vertical. See Tickets lost (parent company blocked). TicketShare (partnership exploration), Shore Tickets. High margins, complex data, white-label interest. Worth continued testing.
3. **SaaS** - Familiar with analytics, often have BI pain. Usually have clean data stacks.
4. **Agencies/Professional Services** - Emerging. Coolr (social media agency, 150 people, COO) validated the fit: multiple siloed tools, no data team, failed Power BI. Worth testing more.
5. **Logistics/Operations** - Gander & White showed strong fit. High-value, operationally complex. Worth continued testing.
6. **AdTech** - Data-heavy, need fast answers.
7. **iGaming** - Deprioritized. BetGames proves we can do it, but data setup is complex and time-intensive. Take inbound only, don't actively prospect.
