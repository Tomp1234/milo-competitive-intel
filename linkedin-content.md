# LinkedIn Content Creator

Creates LinkedIn posts using formulas proven to drive engagement, backed by performance data tracked in the knowledge base.

## Before Writing

Read `linkedin-patterns.md` for current formulas, what works/doesn't, hooks, and question hierarchy. Only load `linkedin-log-full.md` if analysing a specific past post or doing deep performance comparison.

## The Four Winning Formulas

### Formula 1: Timeline Pattern + News Hook (CHAMPION - use first)

```
[Big claim/headline]

The roll out has been going like this:

Month 1: "[Quote/phase]"
Month 3: "[Quote/phase]"
Month 6: "[Quote/phase]"

[News hook - recent announcement/event]

[Insight about what went wrong]

[End with experience question]
```

**Track record:** 2/2 as champion. "Copilot deleted" (126k, 42 comments), "Stop vibe coding" (23.6k, 39 comments). Both organic.

### Formula 2: Story + Quote + Question

```
I [did something specific] yesterday.
[Person] said: "[Real quote]"
[2-3 lines of context]
[Polarizing question that forces a choice]
```

**Track record:** "I cold called Head of BI" - 30 comments.

### Formula 3: Bold Claim + Immediate Quote + "Does this resonate?"

```
[Scary/uncomfortable truth statement]
[Person] told me: "[Real quote]"
[Explanation of why this matters]
Does this resonate?
```

**Track record:** "Your company data is already on ChatGPT" - 14 comments, 158k impressions.

### Formula 4: Bold Accusation + Curiosity Gap + Stats Narrative

```
[Bold accusation directed at reader]
The lie is about [curiosity gap]
[Stats building a narrative - each adds a layer]
[Specific relatable scenario]
[Double question - flip on the reader]
```

**Track record:** "Half your team is lying" - 13.5k, 14 comments. Viable but weaker than F1-F3.

## Content Rules

**Always end with a question.** Use this hierarchy:
1. Debate question: "Volume or quality?" (best)
2. Experience question: "Anyone else seeing this?"
3. Soft question: "Does this resonate?"
4. Never end with a pitch/CTA/link (kills engagement every time)

**Put the best quote in the first 3 lines.** Every time a great quote was buried in the middle, the post underperformed. Lead with the human moment.

**Post Tuesday-Thursday.** Monday and Sunday are death for B2B. Tuesday is the sweet spot.

**Use real quotes from calls.** Check `meeting-patterns.md` for the quote bank. Specific names and titles (anonymised if needed) make posts tangible.

**Match the voice:** Short sentences. Line breaks. Lead with discomfort. No corporate speak. No generic "AI is transforming business" content. Slightly confrontational is good - Benjamin Dennehy style works in content too.

## What Kills Posts (Hard Avoid)

- Statement hooks ("AI is a toy") - 0 comments, 497 impressions
- Generic AI takes vs specific industry critique
- Burying the best quote in the middle
- Ending with pitch/CTA instead of question
- Being relatable without being controversial (no debate = no comments)
- Long posts with no story arc

## Winning Themes

1. **Shadow AI / ChatGPT security** - goldmine, 3 posts all performed
2. **Copilot failures** - punching up at Microsoft always works
3. **Build vs Buy** - challenges builder companies, attracts right prospects
4. **BI Team Trap** - resonates as story, not lecture
5. **Trust gap** - "optimising the query, not giving the trust"
