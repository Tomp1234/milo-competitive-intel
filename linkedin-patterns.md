## The Four Winning Formulas

### Formula 1: Timeline Pattern + News Hook (CHAMPION)

```
[Big claim/headline]

The roll out has been going like this:

Month 1: "[Quote/phase]"
Month 3: "[Quote/phase]"
Month 6: "[Quote/phase]"

[News hook - recent announcement/event]

[Insight about what went wrong]

[End with experience question]
```

**Example:** "Copilot is getting deleted!!" - 126k impressions, 42 comments (ORGANIC)
**Also:** "Stop vibe coding" - 23.6k impressions, 39 comments (ORGANIC)

Timeline pattern is now 2/2 as a champion format. This is the most reliable formula.

### Formula 2: Story + Quote + Question

```
I [did something specific] yesterday.
[Person] said: "[Real quote]"
[2-3 lines of context]
[Polarizing question that forces a choice]
```

**Example:** "I cold called a Head of BI yesterday" - 30 comments

### Formula 3: Bold Claim + Immediate Quote + "Does this resonate?"

```
[Scary/uncomfortable truth statement]
[Person] told me: "[Real quote]"
[Explanation of why this matters]
Does this resonate?
```

**Example:** "Your company data is already on ChatGPT" - 14 comments, 158k impressions

### Formula 4: Bold Accusation + Curiosity Gap + Stats Narrative

```
[Bold accusation directed at reader]

The lie is about [curiosity gap]

[Stat] [Stat] [Contrasting stat]

[Build narrative where each stat adds a layer]

[Specific relatable scenario - "All because someone had a board pack due Friday"]

[Double question - flip it on the reader]
"Are your team doing X? Or are YOU the one doing X?"
```

**Example:** "I bet that half your team is lying to you right now..." - 13.5k impressions, 14 comments (ORGANIC)

## What Drives Comments (Engagement)

| Element | Impact | Evidence |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Polarizing question at end | **Highest** | "Volume or quality?" = 9 comments on 2k impressions |
| Personal vulnerability | High | Sharing bad numbers > sharing good numbers |
| Story format ("I did X") | High | 30 comments on cold call story |
| Quote early (lines 2-5) | High | All winners have this |
| Sarcasm + direct challenge | High | "Your internal build is defiantly the exception though!!" = comment trigger |
| "Does this resonate?" / "Anyone else?" | Medium | Works but softer than debate questions |
| Self-deprecating humor | Medium | "Show me how badly I'm doing" |
| Double question (flip on reader) | Medium-High | "Is your team... Or are YOU..." forces self-reflection |

## What Drives Reach (Impressions)

| Element | Impact | Evidence |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Timeline pattern + news hook | **HIGHEST** | "Copilot deleted" = 126k organic |
| Specific industry hot take | **Very High** | "Self-service BI was a lie" = 31k organic |
| Punching up at Microsoft/big players | Very High | All Copilot posts perform |
| Boosting | High (but dilutes engagement) | 4 days boost = 158k, 3 days = 73k |
| Bold/scary claim | Medium | "Your company data is already on ChatGPT" |
| Trendy terminology | Medium | "Vibe coding" gives cultural relevance |

## What Kills Posts (AVOID)

| Element | Impact | Evidence |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Statement hook (not moment) | **Deadly** | "AI is a toy" = 497 impressions, 0 comments |
| Generic AI take | Deadly | vs specific BI take (497 vs 31,166) |
| Quote buried in middle | Deadly | Ian quote wasted twice |
| Pitch/CTA instead of question | Deadly | "Misinformation factory" = 0 comments |
| Monday posting | Bad | Both flops were Monday |
| Sunday posting | Bad | "Absolutely done" = 764 impressions |
| Can't engage in first hour | Bad | Algorithm needs early velocity |
| No question at end | Bad | Consistently lower engagement |

## Question Hierarchy (Best to Worst)

1. **Debate question:** "Volume or quality?" - forces people to pick a side
2. **Experience question:** "Anyone else seeing this?" - invites stories
3. **Soft question:** "Does this resonate?" - low friction
4. **"Thoughts?"** - lazy but works with great content
5. **No question (pitch/CTA)** - kills engagement

## Boosting Learnings

| Boost Duration | Result |
|----------------|--------|
| 4 days | 158k impressions, 14 comments (0.06% engagement) |
| 3 days | 73k impressions, 30 comments (0.11% engagement) |
| 1 day | 19k impressions, 9 comments (0.18% engagement) |
| No boost (timeline format) | 126k impressions, 42 comments (0.33% engagement) |
| No boost (hot take) | 31k impressions, 3 comments (0.01% engagement) |
| No boost (personal) | 2k impressions, 9 comments (1.0% engagement) |

**Conclusion:** Timeline + news hook format can achieve boosted-level reach organically while maintaining strong engagement. Best strategy: use Formula #1 for maximum organic reach, only boost if needed for brand awareness beyond your network.
