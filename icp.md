# Milo Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Last Updated:** 26 February 2026

---

## The One-Liner

Business leaders at 100-500 employee companies who WAIT for data, have local decision-making authority, and are frustrated by how long it takes to get answers from their data team.

---

## Company Profile

### Size
- **Sweet spot:** 100-500 employees
- **Why:** Large enough to have real data complexity and budget. Small enough that they don't have a 20-person data team building everything in-house.

### Verticals (Ranked by Fit)

| Vertical | Fit | Notes |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **eCommerce/DTC** | #1 Priority | Primary focus. Large addressable market, familiar pain points. |
| **Event Ticketing** | #2 Priority | Emerging vertical. See Tickets lost (parent company blocked). TicketShare, Shore Tickets still in play. High margins, complex data, white-label interest. Worth continued testing. |
| **SaaS** | Strong | Familiar with analytics tools, often have BI pain. |
| **Agencies/Professional Services** | Emerging | Coolr (social media agency, 150 people) - COO strong fit. Multiple siloed tools (Productive, Hi Bob, HubSpot), no data team, failed Power BI. Worth testing more. |
| **Logistics/Operations** | Good | High-value, operationally complex. Worth testing. |
| **AdTech** | Good | Data-heavy, need fast answers. |
| **Defense/Manufacturing** | Test Carefully | Kromek showed interest but defense compliance requirements can block. IP protection angle is strong. Longer sales cycles, PLC procurement. |
| **iGaming** | Inbound Only | BetGames proves we can do it, but data setup complex. Don't actively prospect. |

### Geography Considerations

**Target:** UK/European companies with local decision-making authority

**Disqualify:** UK subsidiaries of US-parent companies where analytics decisions are made in the US.

**Ask early:** "Where's your analytics/BI team based? Who owns those decisions?"

### Primary Buyer: Operations & Innovation Leaders (TARGET THESE)

**Titles (in priority order):**
1. **Head of Innovation / Innovation Director / VP Innovation** - 100% conversion
2. **COO / Chief Operating Officer** - 83% conversion
3. **VP Operations / Director of Operations / Operations Director** - 83% conversion

**What they do:** Run business functions that depend on data but don't manage the data team directly.

**What they say:**

## Qualifying Signals

### Strong Buy Signals

| Signal | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Describes specific workflow problem unprompted | They've thought about this before you called |
| Actively looking at solutions | Move fast |
| Mentions security concerns about ChatGPT | Door-opener - this is already happening |
| Expresses frustration with existing BI tools | "People won't use Power BI" |
| Has operational autonomy | Can make buying decisions locally |
| Mentions Copilot disappointment | Ready for something that actually works |
| Company grown through acquisitions | Data is scattered, need unification |
| Failed Power BI investment | Already validated need AND proven budget exists. Coolr: "spent a huge amount of money... constant manual updating" |
| Budget cycle timing | Evaluating tech stack for next FY = urgency + budget available |
| IP/patent protection concern | For IP-heavy companies (manufacturing, pharma, defense): "can't get a patent on it anymore" |
| Checks integrations during the call | Actively validating fit in real-time - serious buyer |
| Asks "where does it go wrong?" | Due diligence question, not tire-kicking |
| Compares Milo to "having a data team" | Gets the value prop - Coolr: "like having a data engineering team but better" |

### Disqualify Immediately

| Signal | What They Say |
|--------|---------------|
| UK subsidiary of US company | "That's handled by our US team" |
| Large engineering team | "We have engineers who could do this" |
| Technical validator first | "Let me get our CTO to evaluate" |
| No data stack | "We use spreadsheets for everything" |
| Pre-revenue startup | Can't afford, too early |
| **Already has AI solution** | "We use Glean/Claude/Copilot/etc." |
| **Building internally** | "We're pursuing our own initiatives" |
| **Title contains "AI" or "Data"** | 0% conversion rate - skip immediately |
| **Defense/govt compliance requirements** | Can block deals entirely - validate standards early with Philip |
| **"I don't write the checks"** | Engaged champion but not budget holder - expect longer cycle, need decision makers on next call |
| **Parent company governance** | Local team has no buying authority - See Tickets lost despite CEO champion. Ask "who needs to approve this?" early |

## The Disqualification Script

Ask these early:

1. **"Where's your analytics/data team based?"**
   - If US/HQ → "Who there would own a decision like this?"
   - If local → Continue

2. **"Do you have a team that builds your own analytics tools, or do you buy solutions?"**
   - If build → Disqualify
   - If buy → Continue

3. **"Are you already using any AI tools for data questions - like Copilot, Glean, or something internal?"**
   - If yes and satisfied → Disqualify (don't try to displace)
   - If yes but frustrated → Continue (Copilot failures = opportunity)
   - If no → Continue

4. **"Is there anything happening right now that would mean this isn't the right time?"**
   - If major implementation underway → Nurture
   - If clear → Continue

## Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Send me an email" | "I don't know if you really want to - I know a lot of these emails don't get read. Is this genuinely something that could be relevant, or is this a polite way of ending the call?" |
| "We already have Tableau/Power BI" | "That's actually why I'm calling. Most teams have the tools but people still aren't using them because they just want quick answers, not another dashboard to learn." |
| **"We already have an AI solution"** | "Is it actually getting used by business teams, or is it more of an IT tool? Most companies I talk to have something, but the business users still can't get answers without going through someone technical." |
| "Not in my remit" | "Totally understand - who would be the right person to speak to about this?" |
| "We're building our own" | "Got it - sounds like you've got strong internal capability. I'll leave you to it." (Disqualify) |
| "The US handles that" | "Is there someone on the US side I should speak to? Or is this just not something the UK team would be involved in at all?" |

## What Makes Milo Win

### Why Customers Choose Milo

- **Speed:** "Analysis that took business analyst weeks now takes 2 minutes"
- **Accessibility:** Works for non-technical users who won't learn Power BI
- **Explanation:** Answers "why" not just "what"
- **Security:** Data stays controlled, not scattered across personal ChatGPT accounts

### The Bar for Success

Greta (Perlas) defined it: **"Being able to answer questions live in management meetings instead of waiting a week for an analyst."**

### Trust Status (Updated Feb 2026)

Trust improving. BetGames and Perlas check-ins going well - usage expanding across teams. Key goal remains getting to the point where users act on Milo output without double-checking.

## Apollo List Building Rules

### INCLUDE (Priority Order)

1. Head of Innovation / Innovation Director / VP Innovation
2. COO / Chief Operating Officer
3. VP Operations / Director of Operations
4. Commercial Director
5. Director (general business titles)
6. Head of Sales / VP Sales
7. CEO (at companies <200 employees only)

### EXCLUDE (Hard Filter)

- Any title containing "AI"
- Any title containing "Data" as primary function
- Any title containing "BI" or "Business Intelligence"
- Any title containing "Analytics" as primary function
- Head of Engineering / VP Engineering / CTO
- Companies with 50+ engineers on LinkedIn

---

## How to Use This Document

**Before a call:** Check job title against conversion data. If it's a "Head of AI/Data/BI" title, skip or deprioritize.

**During a call:** Listen for qualifying/disqualifying signals. Ask about existing AI solutions early.

**After a call:** Note which pain points landed, which objections came up.

**For SDRs:** This is the filter. Innovation and Operations titles convert. AI/Data/BI titles don't. Period.
