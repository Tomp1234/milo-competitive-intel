## ICP Refinements From These Calls

### Add to Disqualification Signals
- "New to role" + "exploring options" - They're learning the market, not buying
- Security-only driver without BI need - May find Copilot sufficient
- Already hired someone to solve it - Problem being addressed another way
- Recent M&A activity - Budget frozen, org in flux
- VP BI/Analytics title - Confirms 0% conversion pattern
- Defense/security compliance - Can genuinely block, not just an objection. Get specific standards early.
- "I don't write the checks" without path to budget holder - Engaged champion ≠ buyer
- Parent company governance blocking local decisions - See Tickets: strong local fit killed by parent company. Validate buying authority early.

### Add to Buying Signals
- "I'm done with [manual process]" - Emotional investment
- Moving to new/bigger role soon - Wants to look smart walking in
- Can articulate specific use cases - Knows what they need
- Multi-location/multi-unit complexity - Pattern recognition value clear
- CEO direct engagement - Budget holder on the call
- Competitive differentiation driver - "This beats [competitor]"
- Admits ChatGPT guilt unprompted - "I shouldn't say but..." = security angle resonates
- IT ticket bottleneck for data - "Two days minimum" = immediate pain
- Wants "approved AI agent" - Looking for permission to use AI safely
- Failed Power BI investment - Already spent money, proved need, alternative didn't work
- Budget cycle timing - Evaluating tech stack for next FY = urgency + budget available
- Checks integrations during the call - Actively validating fit in real-time
- Asks "where does it go wrong?" - Serious buyer doing due diligence, not tire-kicking
- IP/patent concern from AI usage - Concrete financial risk, not abstract security

## Best Personas From These Calls

1. **Franchise operations leaders** - P&L responsibility, can't build, need patterns across locations
2. **CEOs at mid-market companies** - Budget holder, can move fast when pain is clear
3. **Business/Change Managers** - Operational, sees process improvement, not threatened by AI
4. **COO at services/agency companies** - Owns ops, no data team, multiple siloed systems, budget authority
5. **Head of Innovation** - 100% conversion, tasked with finding solutions, not threatened
6. **NOT new-to-role explorers** - No authority, no urgency
7. **NOT VP BI/Analytics** - Even with genuine pain, wrong persona

## Key Lessons (One-Liners)

- "Sneaking information" = gold framing. Security angle is about giving people permission to use AI without guilt.
- Failed Power BI = best pre-qualification signal. Budget exists, need validated, alternative didn't work.
- CEO + specific differentiation + no objections = fast mover. Don't over-qualify.
- "New to role" + "Just exploring" + "Already have someone working on it" = Nurture, don't pursue.
- Security-only driver without BI need = weak fit. Probe for insight need.
- Recent acquisition = automatic nurture. Don't waste cycles mid-integration.
- Partnership/white-label conversations need different qualification than pain discovery.
- IP/patent protection is the sharpest security angle. "Can't get a patent on it anymore" > generic data security.
- POC user group matters more than POC agreement. Technical-only POCs fail (Raptive pattern).
- COO at 6 months is the sweet spot: enough context to know problems, enough mandate to fix.
