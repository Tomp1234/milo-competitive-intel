# Cold Call Scripts by Persona

Scripts built on Benjamin Dennehy methodology with persona-specific pain points. The structure is always the same - what changes is which pain points you lead with based on who you're calling.

## How to Use

1. **Before dialing:** Identify the persona from your list
2. **Match the script:** Use the pain points that match their role
3. **Follow the structure:** Pattern interrupt > Choice > Pain points > Grinder > Push-away close
4. **After the call:** Use the cold-call-analyzer skill for pattern tracking

## Universal Call Structure

### Opening Pattern Interrupt

> "Hi [NAME], you're probably going to hate me - this is a cold call. Do you want to hang up or give me 30 seconds to tell you why I called?"

This works because it's honest, disarming, and gives them control. Most people will give you 30 seconds out of curiosity.

### If They Give You 30 Seconds

Move to the persona-specific pitch below. The structure is always three pain points framed as what other people in their role tell you, followed by the push-away: "I get the feeling none of these are relevant to you?"

### Push-Away Close (All Personas)

> "Look, I don't know if this is even relevant to you. But if what I've described sounds familiar, it might be worth a 15-minute conversation. If not, tell me to bugger off and I'll leave you alone. What do you think?"

## Script 1: CEO / COO / Commercial Director

They buy because they're frustrated by how long it takes to get answers. They want to answer questions in real-time, not say "let me get back to you."

### 30-Second Pitch

> "I typically get invited in by CEOs and Commercial Directors at mid-market companies.
>
> They tell me they're *frustrated* that when their board asks a question, they have to say 'let me get back to you' instead of answering in the room.
>
> Others say they're *concerned* about employees uploading company data to personal ChatGPT accounts with zero visibility.
>
> And some are just *tired* of waiting days for answers that should take minutes.
>
> I get the feeling none of these are relevant to you?"

### Pain Points to Explore
1. "When was the last time you couldn't answer a question in a meeting because you didn't have the data?"
2. "How long does it typically take to get an answer from your data team?"
3. "Do you know what your team is uploading to ChatGPT?"

### Emotional Grinder Questions
- "How does it feel when you have to say 'let me get back to you' to your board?"
- "What decisions have been delayed because you couldn't get the data in time?"
- "Is this something you've tried to fix before, or have you just accepted it?"

## Script 2: Head of Innovation / VP Innovation / Innovation Director

They buy because they're tasked with finding new ways to work smarter. They need something that delivers, not another pilot that goes nowhere.

**Conversion rate:** 100% (5/5 calls) - Best performing title.

### 30-Second Pitch

> "I typically get invited in by Innovation leaders at mid-market companies.
>
> They tell me they're *frustrated* that despite all the AI hype, their business teams still wait days for answers that should take minutes.
>
> Others say they're *concerned* about employees uploading company data to personal ChatGPT accounts with zero visibility.
>
> And some are just *tired* of piloting AI tools that never get adopted because they don't connect to the company's actual data.
>
> I get the feeling none of these are relevant to you?"

### Pain Points to Explore
1. "What's the biggest gap between your AI ambitions and what's actually being used day-to-day?"
2. "Are people in the business using ChatGPT or Claude with company data already?"
3. "When your team needs a data answer, how long does it actually take?"

### Emotional Grinder Questions
- "How many AI pilots have you run that never made it past the demo?"
- "What happens when the board asks what AI is actually doing for the business?"
- "Is this something you've been trying to solve, or have you accepted it?"

They run the business and feel slow data directly. Manual reporting eats their week.

**Conversion rate:** 83% (5/6 calls)

### 30-Second Pitch

> "I typically get invited in by Operations leaders at mid-market companies.
>
> They tell me they're *frustrated* that business teams still wait days for answers that should take minutes.
>

>
> And some are just *exhausted* from manual reporting - 20 or 30 KPIs every month, pulled from different systems, stitched together by hand.
>
> I get the feeling none of these are relevant to you?"

### Pain Points to Explore
1. "How much of your week goes to pulling together reports that should be automatic?"
2. "When you need an answer from your data, how long does it actually take to get one?"
3. "Are your team using ChatGPT or similar tools with company data?"

### Emotional Grinder Questions
- "How does it feel when you can't answer a question in a meeting because the data isn't there?"
- "What decisions have been delayed because you couldn't get the data in time?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

## Script 4: Head of Sales / VP Sales

They need answers fast to make decisions. They're tired of waiting for reports and flying blind between weekly check-ins.

### 30-Second Pitch

> "I typically get invited in by Sales leaders at mid-market companies.
>
> They tell me they're *frustrated* that getting pipeline or forecast answers takes days instead of seconds.
>
> Others say their team is *wasting time* pulling data from Salesforce into spreadsheets instead of selling.
>
> And some are just *blind* between weekly reports - they don't know what's actually happening until it's too late.
>
> I get the feeling none of these are relevant to you?"

### Pain Points to Explore
1. "How quickly can you answer 'why did we miss target last month'?"
2. "How much time does your team spend pulling reports vs selling?"
3. "When was the last time you were surprised by end-of-quarter numbers?"

### Emotional Grinder Questions
- "What's it cost you to find out about a problem after it's too late to fix?"
- "How do you feel when you can't explain a miss to leadership?"
- "Is this something you've accepted, or are you actively trying to fix it?"

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Send me an email" | "I don't know if you really want me to - I know a lot of these emails don't get read. Is this genuinely something that could be relevant, or is this a polite way of ending the call?" |
| "We already have Tableau/Power BI" | "That's actually why I'm calling. Most teams have the tools but people still aren't using them because they just want quick answers, not another dashboard to learn. Is that something you're seeing?" |
| "We're building our own" | "Got it - sounds like you've got strong internal capability. I'll leave you to it." (Disqualify - walk away) |
| "Not a priority right now" | "That's fair. Can I ask - is it not a priority because you've solved it, or because you've accepted it?" |
| "I don't have time" | "I completely understand. Before I let you go - if you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your team accesses data, what would it be?" |

The pattern across all objection handling: respond with curiosity, not accommodation. Never give a "stupid answer" (defensive, eager to please). Challenge with genuine interest.

---

## Disqualification Triggers

Stop the call early if you hear:

- "We have an engineering team that could build this" - Builder company, walk away
- "Our US/HQ team handles that" - Can't buy locally
- "We use spreadsheets for everything" - No data stack to connect to
- "Let me get our CTO to evaluate the architecture" - Technical validator trap
- "We already use Glean/Claude/Copilot/Metabase and it's working" - Already solved
- "We're pursuing our own initiatives" - Building internally

**Titles to skip entirely (0% conversion from 13 calls):**
Any title containing "AI", "Data", "BI", "Analytics", "Insights" as primary function. If you accidentally reach one, ask for a referral: "Who in your organization is most frustrated by how long it takes to get answers from your data? Operations? Commercial?"
