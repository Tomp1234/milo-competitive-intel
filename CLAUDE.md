## What Milo Is (Use This Language)

**Simple (for non-technical buyers):**
"Milo lets business users ask questions about their data in plain English and get answers with explanations - not just charts."

**Slightly more detail:**
"Milo connects to your existing data stack (BigQuery, Snowflake, Salesforce) and lets anyone ask questions like 'why did revenue drop last week?' without waiting for an analyst. It gives you the why, not just the what."

**The real positioning that wins:**
"ChatGPT for business, but safe" - Companies reach out because they're worried about employees uploading sensitive data to personal ChatGPT accounts. Lead with security concern, not analytics capabilities.

**Key differentiator from traditional BI:**
Companies spend heavily on Tableau/Power BI that show what happened. But business teams still make decisions on guesswork because getting explanations requires days of analyst time. Milo gives the "why" in minutes.

## Sales Methodology - Benjamin Dennehy

I follow Benjamin Dennehy's direct sales approach. This is non-negotiable in how I sell.

**Core principles:**
- Disqualification over qualification - make prospects prove they're worth my time
- Emotional pain discovery, not logical feature discussions
- Challenge prospects rather than accommodate them
- "Be interested and curious, NOT interesting and informative"
- Push-away techniques: "I get the feeling this isn't relevant to you..."
- Pattern interrupts on cold calls
- Never pitch features before uncovering pain

**The Emotional Grinder (discovery framework):**
1. Elaborate / be specific
2. When first recognized
3. Actions taken to fix
4. Cost/impact to business
5. Personal feelings
6. Resignation to status quo

**Objection handling style:**
- Never give "stupid answers" (defensive, accommodating)
- Always respond with curiosity/challenge
- Example: "Send me some information" → "9 out of 10 times that's a polite way of saying 'I want to end this call.' Is that the case here?"

**If I'm pitching features before pain, flag it.**

## Cold Call Structure (Benjamin Dennehy Style)

**Pattern Interrupt:**
"[Name], you're probably going to hate me - this is a cold call. Would you like to hang up now, or give me 30 seconds and then decide?"

**30-Second Pitch:**
"I typically get invited in by Heads of BI and Operations at mid-market companies.

They tell me they're *frustrated* that business teams still wait days for answers that should take minutes.

Others say they're *concerned* about employees uploading company data to personal ChatGPT accounts with zero visibility.

And some are just *exhausted* from their analysts drowning in the same ad-hoc requests over and over.

I get the feeling none of these are relevant to you?"

**If they engage:** Emotional Grinder questions
**If they object:** Curiosity, not accommodation
**Close:** Push-away - "Look, I don't think this is for you..."
